Privacy Policy

Your privacy is our priority. Learn how we handle your data.

Last Updated: November 22, 2025

DocLite ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our website and services (the "Services").

1. Information We Do Not Collect

Our core principle is privacy. All tools on DocLite operate entirely on the client-side, meaning all processing is done within your web browser on your own computer. We do not upload, process, store, or have access to any of your files or the data within them.

  • We do not collect personal data you process with our tools.
  • We do not store your documents, images, or any other files.
  • We do not require you to create an account to use our services.

2. Information We Do Collect

To improve our website and services, we may collect anonymous, non-personal information. This includes:

  • Usage Data: We may collect anonymous data about how you interact with our website, such as which tools are used most frequently, the features accessed, and error occurrences. This data is aggregated and cannot be used to identify you.
  • Device and Browser Information: We may collect standard web log information, such as your browser type, operating system, and device type. This helps us ensure our website functions correctly across different platforms.

3. How We Use Information

The non-personal information we collect is used solely for internal purposes, such as:

  • Analyzing trends to improve our tools and user experience.
  • Diagnosing technical problems and maintaining the security of our services.
  • Understanding user needs to guide the development of new features.

4. Cookies and Third-Party Services

We may use cookies to enhance your experience. Cookies are small files stored on your device that help the website function. You can disable cookies in your browser settings, but this may affect the functionality of some tools.

We may use third-party services like Google AdSense to display advertisements. These services may use cookies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits to our website or other websites. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to our users based on their visit to our sites and/or other sites on the Internet. You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Ads Settings.

5. Data Security

Because we do not collect or store your personal files, the risk of a data breach affecting your personal documents is eliminated. We implement reasonable security measures to protect the anonymous usage data we collect.

6. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child, please contact us to have it removed.

7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes.
